# Audio Plan - Last Shift

## Audio Goals

- Isolation
- Tension
- Mystery
- Final panic

## Audio Categories

| Category | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Ambient hum | Makes the facility feel alive but empty |
| Electrical buzz | Supports broken systems and power objectives |
| Door sounds | Gives doors physical weight |
| Pickup sounds | Confirms important interactions |
| Terminal beeps | Gives system feedback |
| Distant metal impacts | Suggests unseen movement |
| Vent sounds | Adds close, uncomfortable texture |
| Final lockdown alarms | Creates panic during the ending |
| Final entity sound behind player | Suggests danger without showing it |

## Room Audio Cues

### Start Office

- Low room tone
- Faint ventilation
- Occasional fluorescent buzz
- Distant evacuation announcement fragment if available

### Main Corridor

- Long ambient hum
- Distant metal impacts
- Pipe creaks
- Low-frequency rumble after power returns

### Storage Room

- Close vent sound
- Small shelf rattle
- Muffled impact from outside the room

### Fuse Room

- Electrical buzz
- Spark or short circuit sound
- Power restoration thump
- Hum that fades in after activation

### Terminal Area

- Terminal idle beep after power returns
- Monitor static
- Door unlock confirmation sound
- Distant response sound after terminal use

### Control Room / Ending

- Final terminal beep
- Lockdown alarm
- Heavy magnetic door locks
- Red alert siren loop
- Final entity sound behind player
- Abrupt cut or fade to silence

## Implementation Notes

- Keep volume balanced so the player can still understand objective feedback.
- Use triggered sounds sparingly.
- Avoid constant loud scares.
- Let silence do some of the work.
